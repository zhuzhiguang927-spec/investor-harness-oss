# Source And Reliability

Investor Harness does not require visible source-grade labels in user-facing output.

## Current Rule

- Do not add inline source-grade labels to report正文.
- Do not add a source-grade column to tables.
- Do not create standalone sections for source chain, evidence taxonomy, material completeness, or research boundary.
- Use source quality only as an internal judgment aid: distinguish disclosed facts, market views, calculations, and unverified assumptions before writing conclusions.
- Do not list sources for most routine high-confidence data. Company filings, announcements, market quotes, structured financial data, and cross-checked public facts can appear directly.
- If a data point is low-confidence, secondhand/reposted, hard to verify, has conflicting definitions, or is estimated/calculated by the model, state the source,口径, or `估算/测算` right next to that point.
- If a data gap materially changes the conclusion, mention it briefly in the relevant paragraph or in a short final `数据来源与说明`; otherwise keep it internal.

## Writing Standard

Final outputs should read like investment research, not audit logs:

- data first, judgment second;
- explain only the conclusion-relevant source context, especially weak sources, conflicting口径, and self-estimates;
- keep process details out of the report.
